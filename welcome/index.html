Welcome to a class about how a career actually gets built.

Not in a flash. Not by hustling yourself sick. One small habit at a time, until one day you turn around and the career is behind you.

02
Idea 1

Small habits compound.

Nobody graduates and gets handed a career. They get handed a Tuesday. What you do on Tuesday, over and over, is the career.

03
Idea 2

You forget the lecture.

You do not forget the rent.

Real-world stuff sticks because it is real. The first time you negotiate, the first time you file taxes, the first time you say no to a bad gig. We are going to rehearse those moments now, while the stakes are still low.

05
Idea 4

Rejection is not failure.

It is the data.

The audition that did not call back, the email that got no reply, the application that ghosted. You learn the room. You adjust. You send the next one faster. The people who make it are not the ones who get fewer no's.(They are the ones who keep going after them.)

06
Idea 5

Money is a habit, not a number.

Six figures with no system is a slow leak. Thirty-eight hundred a month with a system is freedom. We will build the system this semester. Spreadsheets, yes, but also the small daily moves that make the spreadsheet possible.

07
Idea 6

Boston is your network. Start now.

The room you are sitting in is the highest concentration of future industry people you will ever be in for free. Walk down the hall. Get coffee with the person next to you. The career that starts in May starts with the people you met in January.
